Encryption in SOC 2 Compliance

Encryption is a fundamental security control in SOC 2 compliance. It involves
converting data into an unreadable format to protect it from unauthorized
access. Encryption ensures the confidentiality and integrity of sensitive data
both at rest and in transit.

Encryption at rest refers to protecting data stored on devices or in databases.
Organizations should use strong encryption algorithms such as AES-256 to
encrypt sensitive data stored on servers, laptops, and other devices. Full
disk encryption should be implemented on all devices that store sensitive data.

Encryption in transit refers to protecting data as it travels across networks.
Organizations should use TLS 1.2 or higher to encrypt data transmitted between
systems. All web applications should use HTTPS to ensure that data is encrypted
during transmission.

Key management is a critical aspect of encryption. Organizations must implement
proper procedures for generating, storing, and rotating encryption keys. Keys
should be stored securely and access to keys should be strictly controlled and
monitored.

Auditors assess encryption controls by reviewing encryption policies,
configuration records, and key management procedures. They verify that
encryption is properly implemented and that access to encrypted data is
restricted to authorized personnel. Organizations must provide evidence that
encryption is consistently applied to all sensitive data.
